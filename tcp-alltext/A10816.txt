                  The Concord and the Amitie, two ſhips of worth,
               Whoſe good ſucceſſe all London knoweth:
               In merchant trade where they did vſe to goe,
               Their friends they pleaſd, and did torment their foe:
               What Prizes by the Amitie was brought,
               With Spaniards proud, which their confuſion ſought.
               To all their goods the owners can declare,

               The Suſan Parnell, not leaſt of all this rout,
