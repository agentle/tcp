
      
         
            
            
            By the Kynge and the Queene.
            
               
                  THE QVENES Maieſtie beynge enformed that a number of gentlemen, and others of good hauor, hauinge dwellynge places, and manſion houſes, in diuerſe Shires of the Realme (whoſe abode there, ſpecially at thys tyme of ſeruyce, ſhould be requiſite and neceſſary) haue withdrawen them ſelfes from thence, and lye here in or aboute the citie of London. Do the therefore ſtrayghtlye charge and commaunde, that al and euery ſuch gentlemen, ſhal before the .xx. daye of Apryll next, departe home vnto their dwellynges and manſion houſes, beynge in the ſayd Shires, and there to put them ſelfes in a redynes to ſerue the queenes hyghnes, and the country, as nede and occaſion ſhal requyre, onles they be ſuch as remayne here by ſpecyall order and commaundement, vpon payne of impriſonment, and further fine at her hyghnes pleaſure. Dated at her maieſties manor of Grenewhich the .xxx. day of March.
            
            God ſaue the Kynge and the Quene.
         
      
      
         
            
               EXCVSVM LONDINI IN AEDIBVS Iohannis Cavvodi, Tipographi Regiae Maieſtatis.
            
            
               Anno. M. D. LVIII.
            
            
               Cum priuilegio ad imprimendum ſolum.
